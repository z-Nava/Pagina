CARBON NATURAL

INTRO:

A top-notch burger starts with simple ingredients, and they’re even better when they’re local and sustainable. With beef (and milk!) sourced from Royal Family Farming in Royal City, WA. Regenerative farming is better for the farmers, the cows, the land, and the community.

BUN:

It starts with a high-quality milk bun made from Washington wheat. Wheat is an ideal cover crop as it keeps microbes active near the roots, creating healthy soil that can absorb and store more carbon while maintaining soil health through winter.

PRODUCE:

Burgers topped with local produce have a lower carbon footprint and are tastier. Soil health is vital for healthy crop growth. Rotational farming, where different crops are planted on the same plot over several seasons, helps improve soil health and balance nutrients. Royal grows dozens of crops for greater diversity.

DAIRY:

Don’t forget the cheese! Cows are the ultimate recyclers. They take leftover food waste, which would otherwise end up in a landfill, and convert it into milk. Milk from Royal Dairy heads to Darigold, where it’s crafted into the melty cheese you love.

BEEF:

Fresh, juicy, local beef. Cows create a closed-loop system on farms; planted seeds turn into grassy fields, which cows eat and upcycle to provide high-quality protein as beef. Manure feeds the seeds, giving nutrients back into the soil, restarting the cycle.

HOW IT WORKS

Cows close the carbon cycle

Cows are nature’s recyclers. Everything they eat contributes to a regenerative system. They have four compartments in their stomachs that convert soil-benefiting feed and food waste into nutrient-rich protein, beef, and dairy, all while producing manure that fertilizes crops and restarts the cycle for the next generation of our food. Pretty cool, huh?
